PRODUCT RECOMMENDATIONS

| HOMES

CLASS K
EXTINGUISHER

This type is very important for the kitchen. It is made to put out fires caused by cooking oils, grease, and fats, which can happen while frying food. Water cannot stop these fires and can make them worse, so a Class K extinguisher is the safest and most effective choice for kitchen use.

ABC
EXTINGUISHER

A multi-purpose extinguisher that covers the most common home fire types:
  • Class A: Trash, wood, paper
  • Class B: Flammable liquids (e.g., gasoline)
  • Class C: Electrical fires
Ideal for general home safety.

CARBON DIOXIDE(CO₂)
EXTINGUISHER

This extinguisher is best for electrical fires, such as those caused by computers, TVs, or kitchen appliances. It works by removing oxygen and leaves no messy residue, so it's safe to use around electronics and in clean areas.

ABC
EXTINGUISHER

This is the most useful and versatile fire extinguisher for schools, offices, and public buildings. It can handle fires caused by paper, electrical faults, and flammable liquids, making it perfect for general use in classrooms, offices, labs, and storage areas.

WATER
EXTINGUISHER

Important for basic fires involving things like paper, wood, or cloth. It's simple to use and very effective in places like libraries, classrooms, or hallways, where these materials are common.

CARBON DIOXIDE(CO₂)
EXTINGUISHER

This extinguisher is essential in computer rooms, science labs, or offices with electrical equipment. It is used for electrical fires and leaves no residue, making it safe for expensive devices and areas where cleanliness is important.

FOAM
EXTINGUISHER

Foam extinguishers are great for areas where flammable liquids are used or stored, such as science labs or maintenance rooms. They not only stop the fire but also prevent it from starting again by forming a protective layer.

FIRE SAFETY TOOLS

ALARM BELL
An alarm bell is a loud audible device that sounds when a fire or emergency is detected. It is activated by the fire alarm system or manual call points to alert building occupants to evacuate immediately. Its loud ringing ensures the warning reaches everyone in the area quickly and effectively.

BREAK GLASS
EMERGENCY
KEY BOX
A wall-mounted safety box that holds an emergency key, usually used to unlock fire exits, control panels, or restricted areas during emergencies. The glass panel must be broken to access the key quickly in urgent situations, helping responders take immediate action without delay.

SMOKE
DETECTOR
An electronic device that automatically senses the presence of smoke, a key indicator of fire. When smoke is detected, the alarm sounds to alert occupants, allowing them to evacuate early and safely. Smoke detectors are essential for early fire detection in both residential and commercial spaces.

FIRE
BLANKET
A safety item made of fire-resistant fabric, used to smother small fires or wrap around a person if their clothing catches fire. It cuts off the fire's oxygen supply, extinguishing flames effectively. Fire blankets are often found in kitchens and laboratories.

FIRE HOSE
REELS
A mounted reel containing a long hose connected to a water supply, used by trained personnel to fight fires. It provides a steady stream of water and is usually located in strategic areas of commercial or industrial buildings.

FIRE
EXTINGUISHERS
A portable device designed to control or extinguish small fires in emergency situations. It works by releasing an extinguishing agent such as water, foam, dry chemical powder, or carbon dioxide (CO₂), depending on the type of fire. Fire extinguishers are commonly found in homes, offices, schools, and public buildings.
